If you're moving out of a London rental, the single biggest variable in getting your deposit back is the standard of the final clean. Letting agents and inventory clerks audit against a fixed checklist, and the gap between "I cleaned it myself last weekend" and "passes inventory" is wider than most tenants realise.

This guide gives you 2026 London prices by property size, explains what should be included, and flags the four things that account for almost every cleaning-related deposit deduction.

## London end of tenancy cleaning prices in 2026

These are realistic prices for a single professional clean in zones 1–4 of London. Outer boroughs are 5–10% cheaper. Central addresses with parking restrictions are 5–10% more.

| Studio | £160 – £220 | From £180 |

| 3 bedroom | £300 – £420 | From £342 |
| 4+ bedroom | £380 – £550 | From £432 |

Three things move the price within those ranges:

1. **Property condition.** A flat that's been kept tidy and cleaned regularly takes 30–40% less time than one where months of grease have built up in the kitchen.
2. **Carpets.** Most quotes don't include carpet steam cleaning. If your tenancy started with professionally cleaned carpets, the inventory clerk will check. Carpet cleaning typically adds £40–£100.
3. **External windows.** Internal window cleaning is standard. External windows on flats above ground floor are usually not included; if the inventory listed them as cleaned at check-in, you may need them done separately.

## What an end of tenancy clean should include

Any quote under £150 for a 1-bed in London is probably skipping at least one of these. Confirm in writing before booking.

- Full kitchen deep clean including inside the oven, behind appliances where movable, extractor and filter, hob, splashbacks
- Bathroom descale of taps, showerhead, glass screens, tiles and grout
- Internal windows, sills, frames and tracks
- All internal woodwork: skirting boards, door frames, doors, switches and sockets
- Cupboards and drawers wiped inside and out
- Carpets vacuumed thoroughly (steam clean usually separate)
- Hard floors mopped and sanitised
- Light fittings dusted, accessible high-level surfaces wiped

The bare minimum a clean should include is "everything visible in the inventory check-in report, returned to that state". If the check-in mentioned the oven was professionally cleaned, the oven needs to be professionally cleaned at check-out — that single item is responsible for an unbelievable number of deposit disputes.

## The four things that cause most deposit deductions

In a year of speaking to London letting agents, four things come up repeatedly:

1. **The oven.** Number one by a wide margin. Self-clean cycles don't pass inventory. A professional oven clean takes 90 minutes with the right tools — much longer with the wrong ones.
2. **Limescale in the bathroom.** Tap heads, showerheads, glass screens. London has very hard water; if you haven't descaled in 12 months, the buildup is visible and inventory clerks will flag it.
3. **Inside cupboards.** Top shelves, drawer runners, the bit behind the door. Easy to miss, always checked.
4. **Carpets.** Vacuumed isn't the same as steam-cleaned. Check your check-in report before booking.

## Do you need a "guaranteed" clean?

Look for "48-hour re-clean guarantee" or similar. It means if the agent flags anything on inventory return, the cleaning company comes back free of charge. This is now an industry-standard offer in London — if a company doesn't offer it, that's a meaningful signal.

The guarantee is more important than the price. A £140 clean that fails inventory and forces you to pay another £200 to fix it cost you £340 — more than the £180 you'd have spent on a properly insured, guaranteed clean to begin with.

## A practical tenant checklist

If you want to handle some of the prep yourself before the cleaners arrive:

- Empty the property completely — including under beds, on top of wardrobes, inside cupboards
- Remove all rubbish from inside the property (cleaners aren't a waste removal service)
- Take meter readings and photograph them
- Take date-stamped photos of every room after the clean — these are your evidence in a deposit dispute
- Confirm what the check-in report listed as "professionally cleaned" and book that to be done
